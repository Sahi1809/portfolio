Smart Plant Monitoring & Irrigation

Automated IoT system with sensor-driven logic for real-time plant monitoring. Published in J. Technische Sicherheit (2025).

Arduino IDEIoTEmbedded C

Research & Publication Details: Formally published in the international engineering journal J. Technische Sicherheit (2025).

Overview: This autonomous framework delivers continuous plant health diagnostics and sensor-driven agricultural automation. The system monitors environmental variables in real time, executing target responses with minimal human intervention to prevent crop damage and resource waste.

Hardware Architecture:

  • Sensors: High-precision Soil Moisture probes, PIR motion sensors, Rain sensors, and Color sensors for leaf diagnostics.
  • Power & Regulation: Dual Transformers coupled with stable Voltage Regulators ensuring clean power delivery.
  • Actuation Mechanics: Submersible DC Pump for irrigation loops, Relays for isolation, and a high-torque DC Motor for physical structural adjustments.
  • Communication Stack: Dedicated GSM Module for remote SMS alerts and notifications.

System Logic & Operational Testing Cases:

  1. Case 1 (Intrusion Detection): The PIR sensor continuously sweeps the perimeter to identify movements. When pests or target predators are detected near the crop canopy, the module filters ambient noise, triggers a localized alert, and transmits an immediate notification via GSM to the authorized operator.
  2. Case 2 (Autonomous Irrigation): The soil moisture matrix reads raw telemetry values. If the moisture index falls below the preset threshold (e.g., 30%), the controller opens the digital relay line to activate the DC water pump. The loop terminates automatically when optimal saturation is restored to avoid waterlogging.
  3. Case 3 (Leaf Health & Treatment): An optical color sensor tracks deviations in foliage tint to locate dry or deteriorating leaves. Upon detecting an unhealthy signature, the controller drives an automated sprayer system to apply targeted pesticides while sending a real-time status update to the user.
  4. Case 4 (Weather Protection): When moisture bridges the conductive traces on the rain sensor, the controller signals the DC motor to close a protective canopy shed over the delicate setup. Once rainfall ceases and the sensor plane dries, the system autonomously retracts the shed to resume regular sun exposure.

Fire Fighting Robot

Autonomous fire-detection and suppression robot using flame sensors and control logic. Published in IJAEMA (2024).

Arduino IDEIoTEmbedded Systems

Research & Publication Details: Formally published in the peer-reviewed engineering journal IJAEMA (2024).

Overview: The Firefighting Robot showcases an effective autonomous mitigation blueprint. The hardware components interface seamlessly with the central Arduino controller to analyze sensor inputs dynamically and execute precise steering updates and target suppression routines safely without manual oversight.

Hardware Architecture:

  • Microcontroller: Arduino UNO core parsing sensory configurations.
  • Power & Actuation: 12V Battery source paired with an L293D IC motor driver handling high-current DC Motors.
  • Suppression Mechanics: Submersible DC Pump routed via a digital isolation Relay module.
  • Navigation Arrays: High-sensitivity infrared Fire Sensors mounted at both front corners alongside a custom directional Servo Motor module.
  • Telemetry: Liquid Crystal Display (LCD) providing immediate sensor diagnostic outputs.

System Logic & Operational Testing Cases:

  1. Case 1 (Idle Sweep): If the fire sensors do not pick up thermal anomalies in any direction, the system defaults to scanning. The telemetry panel explicitly reports status metrics as FL: OFF (Fire Left) and FR: OFF (Fire Right).
  2. Case 2 (Left Vector Action): When a heat signature registers on the left front perimeter, the navigation subsystem executes a precise angular turn toward the vector. Telemetry indicates Left Sensor: ON / Right Sensor: OFF.
  3. Case 3 (Right Vector Action): When a signature is acquired on the right front corner, control algorithms reverse the relative wheel speeds to swing towards the source. Telemetry updates to Right Sensor: ON / Left Sensor: OFF.
  4. Case 4 (Target Suppression): Once positioned squarely in front of the hazard, the target nozzle activates via the digital relay line, using its fluid containment structure to autonomously suppress the source completely.

Conclusion & Results: These structured scenarios prove the architecture's potential as a highly efficient, reliable, and entirely autonomous mobile hazard control method suited for dangerous environments.
